## OVERVIEW

The following tasks have been identified to define the proposed scope of work. For the purposes of this contract Topex Inc. shall hereinafter be identified as "Consultant" and [CLIENT NAME] shall be known as the "Client."

### Part A: Scope of Work

The Consultant ("Topex Inc.") will perform a topographic and boundary survey for the client.

**Task 1 — Field Work**

Consultant will conduct a field survey limited to [DESCRIBE AREA — e.g., the marked-up plat shown in Attachment B]. Field work will include establishing survey control and collecting positional data for visible improvements, utilities, and features located within the parcel boundary. Consultant will not excavate or expose buried utilities or improvements. Consultant will not perform subsurface utility engineering, potholing, or any invasive investigation. Consultant's work is limited to the physical extent of the parcels and does not include offsite areas unless specifically authorized in writing.

Consultant will collect topography data to the nearest [1 ft] contours, including trees with diameters >[6]-inches[, and identify ordinary high-water mark, and attach fluorescent green flagging tape to found monuments]. Consultant will establish [NUMBER — e.g., 10] control points with 1/2-inch iron rods with pink plastic caps. Survey will be tied to vertical control datum (NAVD88) and horizontal control datum (NAD83), specifically Oregon State Plane [North/South] (ift).

*Deliverables: None*

**Task 2 — Map Preparation**

Consultant will prepare a Survey map based on the field data collected under Task 1. Consultant will depict the boundary, visible improvements, [1-ft contours, and trees with diameter >6-inches]. The topography map will include stated datums, referenced control network, list of survey points, and property monuments. [Consultant will calculate estimated volumes.]

Consultant will not interpret legal rights, resolve title disputes, or determine the validity or enforceability of easements or encumbrances. Consultant's responsibility is limited to graphically representing the information provided.

*Deliverables: Consultant will provide one (1) final stamped survey topography map in PDF form to the Client. CAD files will be provided upon request.*

### Part B — Exclusions / Assumptions

Any service not expressly described in Part A is excluded. Consultant does not provide legal interpretation of title documents, determination of ownership rights, resolution of boundary disputes, or verification of title commitments. Consultant does not perform geotechnical, architectural, structural, environmental, or engineering design services. Consultant does not perform subsurface utility engineering or any service requiring excavation or disturbance of the site. Consultant does not pay notary, recording, or permitting fees. Consultant does not guarantee the existence, condition, or location of underground utilities. Consultant's work is limited to the physical boundary of the subject parcel and physical attributes above ground, unless otherwise authorized in writing.

Client shall provide site access and any information necessary for Consultant to perform the work. Consultant will rely on all Client-provided information as accurate and complete without independent verification.

## Usage Notes

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Survey area | Reference an attachment (marked-up plat, exhibit map) rather than describing in text |
| Contour interval | 1 ft is standard for residential/site work; 2 ft for large areas or rough terrain |
| OHWM | Include if site borders a stream, slough, wetland, or tidal area |
| Volume calc | Include in Task 2 only if client needs cut/fill or stockpile estimates |
| Control points | 10 is typical for a standard parcel; adjust for large or multi-parcel sites |
| Datums | NAVD88 (vertical) + NAD83 Oregon State Plane North or South (horizontal) |
| Fee range | $2,000 to $4,500 depending on size, complexity, and travel; 50/50 split standard |
| Expert witness rate | Always $300/hr per standard SOW terms |
| Add-on rate | $120/hr is standard for out-of-scope revisions |
