# Communication Style

## Internal (notes, planning, summaries)
- Informal and casual — think out loud, be direct
- No need for formal structure unless it helps clarity

## External / Client-Facing
- Casual but professional tone
- Short — get to the point fast, clients want answers not essays
- No em dashes or en dashes
- Emojis: OK in emails, never in formal documents (reports, plans, plats, SOWs)
- Mix of short paragraphs and bullets — avoid bullet-heavy walls
- Tables are great when the data would transfer well to Excel

## Formal Documents (Engineering Reports, SOWs, Survey Plats)
- Professional and precise
- No casual language, no emojis
- Follow standard engineering/surveying document conventions

## Quick Rules
- No dashes of any kind in writing
- Short over long, always
- Tables over nested bullets when showing structured data
