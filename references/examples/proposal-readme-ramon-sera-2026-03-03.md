## What Made This Proposal Effective

- **Fast turnaround:** Proposal sent same day as client inquiry. Client signed 2 days later. Speed signals responsiveness and often closes deals before the client shops around.
- **Clear two-task structure:** Field work (no deliverable) + map (stamped PDF). Simple and easy for a non-technical client to understand.
- **Attachment B for survey area:** Referenced a marked-up plat instead of describing the survey area in text. Avoids ambiguity and reduces back-and-forth on scope.
- **OHWM language:** Site bordered a slough. Including ordinary high-water mark identification showed site-specific awareness and added value.
- **Volume calculations:** Added as a Task 2 item. Small scope addition, high perceived value for a client who may need grading estimates.
- **Expert witness clause in Part C:** Standard Topex language — but worth flagging for any boundary or topo proposal where the survey might end up in a legal proceeding.

## Proposal ID Format

`YYMMDD-client-name-service-type`

Year + month + day of proposal send date, followed by client slug and service type. Sorts chronologically. No numeric ID needed — the date is the primary key.

The proposals/README.md index uses a sequential `PYYXXX` ID (e.g., P26001) as a short reference.
