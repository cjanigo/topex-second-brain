## Scope Structure

Two-task structure used here — simple and clean for a standard topo + boundary job:

**Task 1 — Field Work** (no deliverable)
- Survey control establishment
- Positional data for improvements, utilities, visible features
- 1-ft contours
- Trees with diameters >6 inches
- OHWM identification and flagging (fluorescent green tape)
- 10 control points: 1/2" iron rods with pink plastic caps
- Datums: NAVD88 (vertical), NAD83 Oregon State Plane North ift (horizontal)

**Task 2 — Map Preparation** (stamped PDF deliverable)
- Boundary and visible improvements
- 1-ft contours and trees >6"
- Stated datums, control network, survey points, property monuments
- Estimated volume calculations
- CAD files upon request

---

## What Made This SOW Notable

- **OHWM and water feature language** — site borders Pooles Slough; ordinary high-water mark identification was explicitly included
- **Volume calculation** — included in Task 2 scope; useful add-on for sloped or graded sites
- **Two-tax-lot project** — single survey covering two adjacent parcels; fee still treated as one fixed amount
- **Survey area defined by attachment** — kept Task 1 language clean by referencing a marked-up plat (Attachment B) instead of a written metes-and-bounds description
- **Expert witness clause** — standard language in Part C converts Topex to expert witness status at $300/hr if litigation arises — useful model for any SOW where a boundary or topo survey could become a legal exhibit
