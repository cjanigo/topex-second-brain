# SOP — Topographic and Boundary Survey

**Applies to:** Topex Inc. topo + boundary survey projects
**Template:** `templates/survey-sow-topo.md`
**Reference example:** `references/examples/survey-sow-signed-ramon-sera-2026-03-05.md`

---

## When to Use This SOP

Use this SOP for any project where the client needs:
- A map showing existing ground contours (topography)
- Boundary lines located and depicted
- Both combined in a single deliverable

Common triggers: site planning, development feasibility, permit support, volume estimates, coastal/riparian features.

---

## Scoping Checklist

Before writing the SOW, confirm the following with the client:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| What tax lot(s) are being surveyed? | Defines scope boundary; needed for the SOW header |
| Is there an existing plat or aerial to mark up? | Use as Attachment B to define survey area cleanly |
| Are there water features (stream, wetland, slough)? | Triggers OHWM language in Task 1 |
| What contour interval is needed? | 1 ft standard; 2 ft for large/rough sites |
| Are volume estimates needed? | Include volume calc language in Task 2 if yes |
| Does the client need CAD files? | Deliverable is PDF by default; CAD is upon request |
| Is this for litigation or permitting? | Flag for potential expert witness exposure |
| Site access — client-owned or third-party? | Client must warrant authority to grant entry |

## Standard Scope Structure

| Task | Description | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Task 1 — Field Work | Survey control, topo data, boundary features, monuments, OHWM (if applicable) | None |
| Task 2 — Map Preparation | Stamped survey topo map: boundary, improvements, contours, trees, datums, survey points | Stamped PDF; CAD upon request |

## Standard Datums (Oregon)

| Datum | Specification |
|---|---|
| Vertical | NAVD88 |
| Horizontal | NAD83, Oregon State Plane North (ift) or South (ift) depending on project location |

Oregon State Plane North: west-central Oregon (Lincoln, Tillamook, Polk, etc.)
Oregon State Plane South: southern Oregon

---

## Standard Field Work Items

- Establish survey control (minimum 2 control points tied to published monuments)
- Collect positional data: improvements, utilities, visible features
- Collect topography to nearest [1 ft] contours
- Flag and measure trees with diameter >6 inches
- Identify and flag OHWM if water feature present (fluorescent green flagging tape)
- Set control points: 1/2" iron rods with pink plastic caps
- Number of control points: typically 6 to 12 depending on parcel size

---

## Standard Map Content (Task 2)

- Boundary lines with bearing/distance calls
- Visible improvements (structures, fences, utilities)
- Contours at stated interval
- Trees with diameter >6" (location and size)
- OHWM line (if applicable)
- Survey point list
- Stated datums and coordinate system
- Control network diagram
- Property monuments (found and set)
- Scale bar and north arrow
- Topex stamp and signature
- Volume calculations (if in scope)

## Fee Guidance

| Project Type | Typical Range |
|---|---|
| Small residential parcel (under 1 acre, 1 day field) | $1,800 to $2,800 |
| Medium parcel (1 to 5 acres, 1-2 day field) | $2,800 to $4,500 |
| Large or complex site (multi-parcel, water features, difficult access) | $4,500+ |

**Payment terms:** 50% down before work starts, 50% due before final map delivery. This is standard — do not deviate without a specific reason.

**Add-on rate:** $120/hr for out-of-scope revisions
**Expert witness rate:** $300/hr if survey becomes a litigation exhibit

---

## Standard Exclusions (always include)

- Legal interpretation of title documents
- Determination of ownership rights or resolution of boundary disputes
- Geotechnical, architectural, structural, or environmental services
- Subsurface utility engineering or excavation
- Notary, recording, or permitting fees
- Guarantee of underground utility existence or location
- Offsite areas (work limited to subject parcel boundary)

## Execution Checklist

**Before field work:**
- [ ] Signed SOW received
- [ ] 50% down payment received
- [ ] Site access confirmed
- [ ] Tax lot map and existing plat pulled from county records
- [ ] Equipment calibrated and control network planned

**Field work:**
- [ ] Survey control established and tied to published control
- [ ] Boundary monuments found and documented
- [ ] Topography collected at required interval
- [ ] Trees >6" diameter located and sized
- [ ] OHWM flagged (if applicable)
- [ ] Control points set: 1/2" iron rods with pink plastic caps
- [ ] Field notes reviewed before leaving site

**Office / Map Prep:**
- [ ] Field data processed and checked
- [ ] Boundary resolved and drawn
- [ ] Contours generated and reviewed for accuracy
- [ ] Map content complete (see checklist above)
- [ ] Volume calculations completed (if in scope)
- [ ] Map reviewed for datum labels, scale, north arrow, legend
- [ ] Stamped and signed PDF exported

**Delivery:**
- [ ] Final PDF sent to client
- [ ] CAD files sent if requested
- [ ] Final invoice sent for remaining 50%
- [ ] Project README deliverable checklist updated

## Common Issues and Watch-outs

| Issue | Response |
|---|---|
| Client asks for legal boundary opinion | Outside scope — refer to Part B exclusions |
| OHWM is ambiguous or contested | Document what was observed; note in map that OHWM is approximate |
| Client wants revisions after final delivery | Written amendment required; bill at $120/hr |
| Survey is requested as a litigation exhibit | Activate expert witness clause; rate becomes $300/hr |
| Site access denied or restricted | Stop work; notify client per Terms and Conditions Section 6.2 |
